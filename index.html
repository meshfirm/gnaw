Frequently asked questions

Dog owners are searching for ways to help their pets better cope with allergies, digestive problems, hot spots, skin and coat problems and degenerative diseases.
The answer is better food

Is raw/fresh food for my dog?

Dogs are wolfs. Although the dog's appearance has changed greatly, it has kept the same species-specific characteristics of its ancestor. Like the wolf, dogs are also predators - they are carnivores, but not exclusively carnivores.

What are the benefits of fresh food (BARF)?

Biologically Appropriate Raw Food has many benefits including: stronger immune system, improved muscularity, stronger tendons and ligaments, fewer growth problems, less trouble with arthritis,  limited risk of bowel obstruction, healthy and shiny coat, less dog farts, fewer parasite infestations and smaller amounts of excrement

What is the problem with dry dog food?

f you want your pet to be healthy and live a long and active life, it means not feeding your pet cooked and/or processed foods. This means no diets based on cooked grains, dry food - no matter how persuasive the advertising. Don't be lured by the starburst on the front of the bags - "Real Meat" or "All Natural Rice and Chicken Formula”. Read on. Read all the ingredients on the back of the package. Do you understand what they are? Are there preservatives, coloring agents, additives, grains, chemicals or fillers? Has the diet been cooked?

How do I get started with fresh dog food?

Most dogs quickly get accustomed to fresh food. For most dogs, the change of food does not cause problems, and any if the dog does get loose stomach it disappear by itself typically within a week or two.
